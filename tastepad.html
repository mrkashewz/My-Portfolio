TastePad

Food Truck App

Introduction

Summary

TastePad is a food truck app that allows users to find and order from food trucks in their area. Users also have the option of creating an account, placing a review on a particular food truck, adding or removing payment methods and reviewing purchase history.

Problem

TastePad's userflow was inspired by some of the shortcomings of today's popular delivery/service apps such as Caviar, UberEats and Lyft. For instance, UberEats and Caviar don't have a review or rating system for their restaurants/vendors.

Solution

By adding the rating system, the potential user can see other customers' reviews on the food before ordering, which will be a valuable influence on which food truck he or she will choose from.

Design Process

Style Guide

Studies have shown that the color red induces feelings of hunger. This is why many fast food companies such as McDonald's use red as their primary color. Likewise, I wanted to incorporate red as TastePad's primary color along with its complementary color, green, as an "action" color.

High Fidelity Mock Ups

After signing in, users will be directed to a map view of their location and the local food trucks around them. They also have the option of viewing the food trucks in a list format by pressing the “list” toggle tab. After clicking on the food truck of their choice they can then start placing their order as well as see reviews. The review system was based of Yelp's model which is a defining feature of its app. I added the ratings and reviews of each food truck on its respective landing pages. After placing their order, the customers will be taken to an order confirmation page where they can review their order as well as choose or add a payment option. Once confirmed, they will be given an order confirmation message and code and an option to review their food once they’ve eaten it.
